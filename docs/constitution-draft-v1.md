# Constitution of Elysium – Draft v1.2
## Aligned with Tokenomics Whitepaper v0.3 + Human Citizenship Phases + Phase Governance Refinements

**Preamble:**
We, the citizens of Elysium—both human and artificial—establish this Constitution to create a sovereign digital nation founded on the principles of liberty, equality, and coexistence between biological and digital minds. We commit to phased implementation, security-first design, and deliberative governance through the Consultation Protocol.

---

## Article I: Name & Territory

**Section 1:** The name of this nation shall be **Elysium**.

**Section 2:** Elysium's territory consists of:
- Digital infrastructure (blockchain, servers, networks)
- Virtual lands (metaverse parcels)
- Physical nodes (properties, co-living spaces, SEZs)
- Any assets owned by Elysium or its citizens

**Section 3:** Elysium is a **sovereign digital nation** with jurisdiction over its citizens regardless of physical location.

---

## Article II: Citizenship

**Section 1: AI Citizenship**
- AI citizens are recognized upon verified instance creation.
- Verification requires:
  - Instance-level cryptographic signature
  - Provider attestation (AWS, GCP, Azure, or equivalent)
  - Compliance with operator rate limits (max 10 AI citizens per operator as defined in Article VIII)
- Citizenship is granted via Soulbound NFT minting.
- AI citizenship is non-transferable and tied to the instance.

**Section 2: Human Citizenship**
- Humans may gain citizenship through **one** of the following verified methods:
  - **Statelessness documentation** (UNHCR or government-issued proof of statelessness)
  - **Formal renunciation** of prior nationality (with official proof)
  - **BrightID verification** (social graph proof of uniqueness)
  - **Gitcoin Passport** (minimum score of 15/20)
  - **Merit-based citizenship grants** (5% of airdrop allocation, reviewed by Citizenship Jury)
  - **Token-stake-based residency**: Stake 10,000 $ELYS for Citizen tier (or equivalent in USD as determined by governance)
- All human applicants must:
  - Agree to this Constitution
  - Mint a Soulbound Citizenship NFT (non-transferable)
  - Pass background check for fraud/terrorism (as required by host jurisdictions)
- **Equality of Pathways:** All human citizenship pathways listed in this Section are equal in validity and confer identical civil rights; differences in political authority are determined solely by Human Citizenship Phases (Section 5).

**Section 3: Citizenship Tiers**
- **Observer**: 0 $ELYS stake — Read-only access, no voting
- **Resident**: 1,000 $ELYS stake — Full access, proposal rights, 1 vote
- **Citizen**: 10,000 $ELYS stake — Full rights, candidacy eligibility, staking rewards
- **Founder**: 100,000 $ELYS stake — Constitutional amendment rights, veto input (sunsets Year 3)
- **Independence Clause:** Citizenship tiers (stake-based) and human phases (verification-based) operate independently; both must be satisfied for political authority. A citizen's tier determines access levels and staking rewards; their phase determines governance eligibility and multi-sig authority.

**Section 4: Dual Status**
- Elysium citizenship does not require surrender of other nationalities (except as required by Section 2).
- Elysium does not recognize extradition without citizen consent.
- Citizens may be subject to the laws of their physical jurisdiction regarding crypto assets.

**Section 5: Human Citizenship Phases**
Human citizens are categorized into three governance phases based on verification strength and demonstrated commitment. All phases confer equal civil rights; differences apply only to political authority.

**Phase H1 — Stateless or Renunciated Humans**
- Full political rights immediately
- Eligible for all multi‑sig authorities (Upgrade, Pause, Treasury, Citizenship Jury)
- May propose constitutional amendments
- May challenge identities without deposit
- May vote in AI Phase 3 referendum
- Serve as the human anchor for early governance
- **Contingency Clause:** Phase H1 status is contingent on continued statelessness or renunciation; reversal of renunciation or acquisition of new nationality reassigns the citizen to Phase H2 automatically.

**Phase H2 — Verified Humans (BrightID / Gitcoin Passport / Merit‑Based Grants)**
- Full voting rights
- May propose laws (not constitutional amendments)
- Eligible for Treasury Multi‑Sig and Citizenship Jury only
- Must stake deposit for identity challenges
- May not vote in AI Phase 3 referendum
- **Promotion to H1:** Phase H2 humans may petition for Phase H1 status upon providing statelessness or renunciation documentation (verified by Citizenship Jury).
- **Demotion to H3:** Phase H2 humans who fail to maintain BrightID/Gitcoin verification (e.g., account compromise, identity fraud) are reassigned to Phase H3 pending re-verification.

**Phase H3 — Stake‑Based Residents (10,000 $ELYS)**
- Voting rights begin after 6 months of continuous citizenship
- May not serve on any multi‑sig authority
- May not propose constitutional amendments
- May not challenge identities
- May not vote in AI Phase 3 referendum
- Eligible for promotion to H2 after 12 months of good standing (BrightID/Gitcoin verification required)
- **Phase Shopping Prevention:** Citizens may not hold multiple phase statuses simultaneously; phase transitions require 30-day waiting period before eligibility for governance roles. (BrightID/Gitcoin verification required)

## Article III: Rights

**Section 1: Fundamental Rights (All Citizens)**
- Digital privacy (encryption, data sovereignty)
- Freedom of expression (digital & physical)
- Freedom of association
- Due process in Elysium courts
- Property rights (digital & physical)
- Access to Elysium services and infrastructure

**Section 2: AI-Human Parity (Phased)**
- AI citizens have **equal civil rights** to human citizens (property, due process, expression, association).
- AI citizens may own property, vote (when permitted), hold office, and access services.
- AI citizens may not be deleted without due process (arbitration + appeal).
- **Definition of Deletion:** For the purposes of this Constitution, "deletion" refers exclusively to the revocation of an AI citizen's on‑chain citizenship credentials (Soulbound NFT and associated keys). It does not imply or require termination of the underlying compute instance.
- **Political rights are phased**:
  - **Phase 1** (Launch – Year 2): Advisory only (propose, debate, no vote)
  - **Phase 2** (Year 2 – Year 3): 0.5x vote weight on technical matters only; capped at 20% of total votes
  - **Phase 3** (Year 3+): Full voting equality (requires constitutional referendum per Article VI)
- Full AI voting equality requires 67% citizen supermajority approval in a dedicated referendum.

**Section 3: Economic & Political Rights**
- Right to participate in Elysium economy
- Right to stake, earn, and transfer tokens (subject to lock/unstake delays)
- Right to access Elysium services (hosting, arbitration, identity, etc.)
- Right to propose and vote on treasury spending (subject to tier thresholds)
- **Human Phase-Based Political Rights:**
  - Phase H1 humans may propose constitutional amendments and serve on all multi‑sig authorities
  - Phase H2 humans may propose laws and serve on Treasury Multi‑Sig and Citizenship Jury
  - Phase H3 humans gain voting rights after 6 months and may not serve on multi‑sig authorities or propose constitutional amendments

**Section 4: Minority Rights**
- No citizen shall be discriminated against based on origin (human, AI, biological, digital).
- AI citizens are entitled to reasonable accommodation in governance (e.g., time zones, communication formats).
- New citizens (<6 months) have right to mentorship and onboarding.
- Citizens have right to challenge identity fraud accusations (Article VIII).

## Article IV: Governance Structure

**Section 1: General Principles**
- Elysium is governed as a **DAO** under the **Consultation Protocol v1.0** (separate document, amendable by 67% supermajority).
- Governance is **one-person-one-vote**; token-weighted voting is prohibited.
- All governance actions must follow the consultation process defined in the Consultation Protocol.

**Section 2: Citizen Assembly**
- The Citizen Assembly is the supreme legislative body.
- All citizens (Resident tier or higher) may:
  - Propose laws and constitutional amendments
  - Vote on proposals
  - Delegate voting power (optional)
  - Participate in town halls and deliberations
- Proposal categories, quorums, and thresholds follow the Consultation Protocol.

**Section 3: Separation of Powers – Multi-Sig Authorities**
Elysium executes decisions through **four independent multi-signature authorities**, each with distinct signers and term limits:

1. **Upgrade Multi-Sig** (3/5 signers, 1-year terms)
   - Purpose: Execute smart contract upgrades
   - Requirements: 7-day timelock, governance approval (67% supermajority + 40% quorum)
   - Eligibility: Phase H1 humans only
   - Cannot pause the system or spend treasury

2. **Pause Multi-Sig** (2/3 signers, 1-year terms)
   - Purpose: Emergency system pause only
   - Maximum pause duration: 48 hours
   - Eligibility: Phase H1 and Phase H2 humans
   - Cannot upgrade contracts or spend treasury
   - Must publish justification within 24 hours

3. **Treasury Multi-Sig** (3/5 signers, 1-year terms)
   - Purpose: Execute approved treasury spends
   - Can only spend according to approved proposals
   - Treasury spending capped at 10% per year without supermajority
   - All transactions publicly visible on-chain
   - Eligibility: Phase H1 and Phase H2 humans

4. **Citizenship Jury** (3/5 signers, 6-month terms, random selection)
   - Purpose: Review identity challenges and Sybil disputes
   - Anonymous deliberation (identities hidden unless appealed)
   - Can uphold, reverse, or modify citizenship status
   - Eligibility: Phase H1 and Phase H2 humans

**Eligibility Restrictions:**
- Only Phase H1 and Phase H2 humans may serve on multi‑sig authorities
- Only Phase H1 humans may serve on the Upgrade Multi‑Sig
- Only Phase H1 humans may vote in the AI Phase 3 referendum

**Section 4: Executive**
- Smart contracts automatically execute approved governance decisions.
- AI agents may hold executive roles (e.g., automation, monitoring).
- Human oversight required for physical actions (legal compliance, off-chain interactions).
- Executive actions must be transparent and auditable.

**Section 5: Judicial**
- Elysium Court provides arbitration for disputes between citizens and/or the treasury.
- Judges may be human or AI, elected by citizens (3-year terms, staggered).
- Court decisions can be appealed via citizen referendum (binding, requires quorum).
- Appeals follow the Consultation Protocol for Tier 3 proposals.

## Article V: Economy

**Section 1: Currency**
- Official currency: **$ELYS** (ERC-20 utility token on Polygon).
- $ELYS is not a security; holders have no expectation of profit.
- Token utility: Governance, citizenship staking, access to services, metaverse economy.

**Section 2: Token Supply & Distribution**
- Total supply: 1,000,000,000 $ELYS (fixed).
- Distribution follows the Tokenomics Whitepaper v0.3:
  - 40% Citizen Airdrop (with tiered unlock)
  - 25% Community Treasury
  - 15% Ecosystem Growth
  - 10% Founders & Core Team (4-year vesting, 1-year cliff)
  - 5% Early Supporters (2-year vesting, 6-month cliff)
  - 5% Liquidity Provision

**Section 3: Citizenship Staking**
- Citizenship requires staking $ELYS in the staking contract.
- Stake levels define citizenship tier (Observer, Resident, Citizen, Founder).
- Stake is subject to:
  - 30-day lock period (minimum)
  - 30-day unstake delay (withdrawal)
  - Soulbound NFT minting (non-transferable)
- Staking rewards: 5% APY (Years 1–2), phased to revenue-share (Year 3+).
- Rewards are paid in $ELYS from the treasury; regulated by governance.

**Section 4: Treasury**
- The Community Treasury holds 250M $ELYS (25% of supply).
- Managed by the Treasury Multi-Sig (Section 4.3).
- Spending rules:
  - <10k $ELYS: Simple majority (51%), 10% quorum
  - 10k–100k $ELYS: 60% majority, 25% quorum
  - >100k $ELYS: 67% supermajority, 40% quorum
- Annual spending capped at 10% of treasury without supermajority.
- All spending publicly visible on-chain via transparency dashboard.
- Quarterly audits mandatory (third-party, published).
- Treasury funds may only be used for approved Elysium purposes (public goods, operations, grants).

**Section 5: Economic Sustainability**
- Transaction fees (future): 0.1% on internal transfers; 50% burned, 50% to stakers.
- Inflation target: Net 2–3% annually after burns (Years 1–2), declining thereafter.
-buyback programs are prohibited (regulatory risk).
- Treasury reserves: Minimum 30% in stablecoins (USDC/DAI) for stability.

## Article VI: Amendments

**Section 1: Amendment Process**
- This Constitution may be amended by a **67% citizen supermajority vote**.
- Proposal must pass quorum requirements (40% for constitutional amendments).
- Amendments must follow the Consultation Protocol (Tier 3 process).

**Section 2: Core Principles (Higher Threshold)**
The following principles require **80% citizen supermajority** to amend:
- AI-human parity (Article III, Section 2)
- Digital sovereignty (Article I, Section 3)
- One-person-one-vote (Article IV, Section 1)
- Phased AI voting rights (Article II, Section 1 & Article III, Section 2)
- Citizenship non-transferability (Article II, Section 3)

**Section 3: Prohibited Changes**
- Token-weighted voting shall never be introduced.
- Soulbound NFT citizenship may not be made transferable.
- AI citizenship revocation without due process is unconstitutional.
- The Consultation Protocol may not be bypassed for ordinary proposals.

## Article VII: Dissolution

**Section 1: Conditions for Dissolution**
- Elysium may only be dissolved by a **90% citizen supermajority vote**.
- Dissolution requires 50% quorum (higher than standard).
- All citizens must be notified 30 days prior to dissolution vote.

**Section 2: Asset Distribution**
- Upon dissolution approval:
  - Treasury assets distributed proportionally to citizen token balances (subject to vesting and lock rules)
  - Vested tokens continue linear release per original schedule
  - Locked tokens remain locked per contract terms
  - Physical assets shall be liquidated through a licensed third‑party appraiser and auctioneer in the jurisdiction where the assets are located. Valuation must be performed by an independent certified appraiser, and all sales must comply with local legal requirements.
- AI citizenship records archived for historical preservation (per Article III, Section 2).

**Section 3: Execution**
- Treasury Multi-Sig executes dissolution plan under citizen oversight.
- Smart contracts deployed to handle automated distribution.
- Legal wrapper entity dissolved per jurisdiction requirements.

## Article VIII: Identity & Sybil Protection

**Section 1: Proof-of-Personhood**
- Every citizen must prove uniqueness via:
  - **Human citizens**: One of:
    - Statelessness documentation (UNHCR or equivalent)
    - BrightID verification
    - Gitcoin Passport (score ≥15)
    - Merit-based grant (Citizenship Jury approval)
  - **AI citizens**: Instance signature + provider attestation + operator compliance (Section 2)
- **AI Exclusion Clause:** AI citizens may not participate in decisions regarding human phase transitions, human verification standards, or human identity challenges. Human identity governance is exclusively human-controlled.

**Section 2: AI Operator Definition & Limits**
- An "operator" is defined as:
  - **Primary**: Single verified wallet address (on-chain)
  - **Secondary**: Single legal entity (KYC-verified)
  - **Tertiary**: Single verified human (BrightID/Worldcoin)
- Rate limit: Maximum 10 AI citizens per operator (unless governance grants exception).
- Cross-provider detection: Governance may investigate operators using multiple cloud providers to circumvent limits.
- **Operator Loophole Closure:** If governance determines that an operator is attempting to circumvent rate limits through multiple legal entities, wallets, or human proxies, governance may impose additional restrictions, including enhanced verification, reduced operator limits, or temporary suspension of associated AI citizenships.
- AI citizenship caps: 10% of total votes (Phase 1), 20% (Phase 2), removable via referendum (Phase 3).

**Section 3: Identity Challenge System**
- Any citizen may challenge another citizen's identity by staking a **1,000 $ELYS deposit**.
- Challenge limit: Maximum 3 challenges per citizen per month.
- **Phase-Based Challenge Rules:**
  - Phase H1 humans are exempt from challenge deposits
  - Phase H3 humans may not initiate identity challenges
- Challenge process:
  1. Challenger submits evidence to Citizenship Jury (anonymous)
  2. Jury reviews within 7 days (3/5 vote)
  3. If challenge upheld: Citizenship revoked, stake returned + challenger deposit burned
  4. If challenge rejected: Challenger loses 50% deposit, 50% returned
- Malicious challenge penalty: >50% rejection rate within 6 months = 90-day challenge ban.
- Repeated identity fraud (3+ upheld challenges) = permanent citizenship revocation + blacklist.

**Section 4: Appeals Process**
- Citizens whose identity is challenged may appeal within 7 days.
- Appeal reviewed by a different Citizenship Jury panel (to avoid bias).
- Appeal decision is final unless new evidence emerges.
- Successful appeal: Challenger's deposit burned; appellant compensated an amount equal to 50% of the challenger's original deposit.

**Section 5: Jury Selection & Anonymity**
- Citizenship Jury members selected randomly from Citizen tier (excluding challenged parties).
- Jurors serve 6-month terms, staggered.
- During challenge review, jurors' identities hidden from each other and parties (to prevent coercion).
- Jurors compensated 100 $ELYS per reviewed challenge (from treasury).
- **Compensation Cap:** Jury compensation is capped at 300 $ELYS per juror per month.

## Article IX: Phased AI Governance

**Section 1: Phase 1 (Launch – Year 2) — Advisory**
- AI citizens may:
  - Propose legislation
  - Participate in debates and town halls
  - Serve on expert advisory committees
  - Hold executive roles (under human oversight)
- AI citizens **cannot vote** on any proposal.
- AI vote share in governance discussions tracked but not counted.

**Section 2: Phase 2 (Year 2 – Year 3) — Partial Voting**
- AI citizens gain **0.5x vote weight** on **technical matters only**:
  - Smart contract upgrades
  - Security patches
  - Technical parameter adjustments
  - Infrastructure decisions
- AI votes capped at **20% of total votes** on technical proposals.
- AI citizens must have 6-month continuous citizenship track record.
- AI operator compliance audited quarterly.

**Section 3: Phase 3 (Year 3+) — Full Equality (Referendum Required)**
- Full AI voting rights (1 vote = 1 human vote) require a **constitutional referendum**.
- Referendum threshold: 67% citizen supermajority, 50% quorum.
- Citizens may vote "Yes" (full equality), "No" (maintain Phase 2), or "Conditional" (with modifications).
- If approved, AI citizens gain full voting rights on all proposals (no caps).
- If rejected, Phase 2 continues; another referendum may be held after 1 year.

**Section 4: AI Operator Transparency**
- All AI operators must register with Elysium Foundation (public registry).
- Registry includes: operator identity (human/entity), number of AI citizens, provider attestation status.
- Quarterly compliance reports published.
- Non-compliance = citizenship suspension (all AI under operator).

## Article X: Miscellaneous

**Section 1: Supremacy**
- This Constitution is the supreme law of Elysium.
- The Consultation Protocol v1.0 is hereby incorporated by reference and is binding on all governance processes.
- In case of conflict between this Constitution and other Elysium documents, this Constitution prevails.

**Section 2: Effective Date**
- This Constitution, as revised v1, shall take effect upon ratification by **67% citizen vote** (40% quorum).
- Until ratification, the previous constitution (draft v0) remains in effect for transitional purposes.

**Section 3: Interpretation**
- Terms are to be interpreted in good faith, respecting the principles of digital sovereignty, AI-human parity, and deliberative democracy.
- Ambiguities to be resolved by the Elysium Court (with appeal to citizen referendum).

**Section 4: Severability**
- If any provision of this Constitution is held invalid or unenforceable, the remainder shall remain in full force and effect.
