# Elysium Consultation Protocol v1.1
## Deliberative Governance Framework

**Version:** 1.1  
**Date:** 2026-02-27  
**Status:** Community Review + Governance Vote Pending  
**Constitutional Reference:** Article IV (Governance) — Supplementary Protocol

---

## 📋 Executive Summary

This protocol establishes **mandatory consultation and deliberation processes** for all Elysium governance proposals, ensuring informed decision-making, minority protection, and thoughtful governance.

**Core Principle:** *Vote only after listening.*

---

## 🎯 Purpose

The Consultation Protocol exists to:

1. **Prevent Rushed Decisions** — Mandatory review periods before votes
2. **Ensure Informed Voting** — Citizens receive full context and expert analysis
3. **Protect Minorities** — Structured input from affected groups
4. **Surface Expertise** — Technical and legal review before implementation
5. **Build Consensus** — Deliberation before division (voting)
6. **Maintain Stability** — Cooling-off periods prevent whiplash

---

## 👥 Human Citizenship Phases Integration

Human citizens are categorized into three phases (H1/H2/H3) per Constitution Article II. All phases have equal civil rights and may participate in consultations; differences apply only to governance leadership roles.

**Phase H1 (Stateless/Renunciated):**
- May submit proposals (all tiers)
- May serve as moderators, town hall facilitators, expert reviewers
- May initiate tier classification challenges
- May write final summaries
- Count toward minority veto thresholds

**Phase H2 (Verified: BrightID/Gitcoin/Merit):**
- May submit proposals (all tiers)
- May serve as moderators, town hall facilitators, expert reviewers (Treasury + Ethics committees only)
- May initiate tier classification challenges
- May write final summaries
- Count toward minority veto thresholds

**Phase H3 (Stake-Based Residents):**
- May submit proposals (all tiers, subject to spam limits)
- May participate fully in discussions and Q&A
- May NOT serve as moderators or town hall facilitators
- May NOT initiate tier classification challenges
- May NOT write final summaries
- Do NOT count toward minority veto thresholds

**AI Citizens (Phase 1 — Advisory):**
- May contribute analysis and commentary in discussions
- May serve as non-voting advisors to expert committees
- May NOT serve as moderators or town hall facilitators
- May NOT classify proposals
- May NOT write final summaries
- May NOT initiate tier classification challenges
- May NOT count toward minority veto thresholds

**Rationale:** Governance leadership anchored in trusted humans (H1/H2) while maintaining inclusive participation for all citizens.

## 📊 Proposal Classification

Not all proposals require the same consultation level. Elysium uses a **3-tier system**:

| Tier | Category | Examples | Consultation Required |
|------|----------|----------|----------------------|
| **Tier 1** | Simple / Operational | Parameter tweaks, small treasury spends (<100k $ELYS), partnerships | Minimal (3 days) |
| **Tier 2** | Significant / Policy | New programs, medium treasury spends (100k–1M $ELYS), governance changes | Standard (7 days) |
| **Tier 3** | Constitutional / Critical | Constitutional amendments, large treasury spends (>1M $ELYS), citizenship rules, AI voting rights | Comprehensive (14+ days) |

**Classification Disputes:** Only Phase H1 and H2 humans may initiate tier classification challenges (prevents spam from low-trust actors). Resolved by 3/5 Citizenship Jury within 48 hours.

**Proposal Spam Prevention:** Citizens may submit a maximum of 3 proposals per 30-day period unless granted an exception by the Governance Committee (prevents system overload from malicious actors).

## 🗓️ Consultation Timeline (By Tier)

### Tier 1: Minimal Consultation (3 Days)

| Phase | Duration | Requirements |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Publication** | Day 0 | Proposal posted to governance forum |
| **Comment Period** | Days 1–3 | Citizens can comment, ask questions |
| **Author Response** | Day 3 | Proposal author addresses key questions |
| **Vote** | Day 4+ | Voting opens (7-day voting period) |

**Applies To:**
- Treasury spends <100k $ELYS
- Parameter adjustments (e.g., staking APY ±1%)
- Non-controversial partnerships
- Operational decisions

---

### Tier 2: Standard Consultation (7 Days)

| Phase | Duration | Requirements |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Publication** | Day 0 | Proposal + rationale posted to forum |
| **Community Discussion** | Days 1–5 | Open forum discussion, Q&A |
| **Town Hall #1** | Day 3 or 4 | Live voice/text town hall (recorded) |
| **Expert Review** | Days 5–6 | Technical/legal review (if applicable) |
| **Author Revision** | Day 6 | Author can revise proposal based on feedback |
| **Final Summary** | Day 7 | Pro/con summary published |
| **Vote** | Day 8+ | Voting opens (7-day voting period) |

**Applies To:**
- Treasury spends 100k–1M $ELYS
- New governance programs
- Citizenship policy changes (non-constitutional)
- AI advisory council appointments
- Ecosystem grants >50k $ELYS

---

### Tier 3: Comprehensive Consultation (14+ Days)

| Phase | Duration | Requirements |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Publication** | Day 0 | Full proposal + legal/technical analysis |
| **Community Discussion** | Days 1–10 | Open forum, structured debate (pro/con/rebuttal) |
| **Town Hall #1** | Day 3 | Live voice/text town hall (recorded, transcribed) |
| **Expert Review Period** | Days 4–8 | Technical + legal advisory committees review |
| **Expert Reports Published** | Day 9 | Non-binding recommendations public |
| **Town Hall #2** | Day 10 | Q&A with experts + proposal authors |
| **Author Revision** | Days 11–12 | Author can revise based on feedback |
| **Final Summary** | Day 13 | Neutral summary of positions, trade-offs |
| **Cooling-Off** | Day 14 | No new arguments, reflection period |
| **Vote** | Day 15+ | Voting opens (10-day voting period for Tier 3) |

**Applies To:**
- Constitutional amendments
- Treasury spends >1M $ELYS
- Citizenship eligibility rules
- AI voting rights changes
- Core principle modifications (Article VI, Section 2)
- Dissolution proposals

## 🏛️ Consultation Infrastructure

### 1. Governance Forum

**Platform:** Discourse, Commonwealth, or dedicated governance forum

**Requirements:**
- Threaded discussions (pro/con/rebuttal)
- Upvote/downvote on arguments (not proposals)
- Citation system (link to sources, evidence)
- Searchable archive of all past consultations
- AI moderation for civility and spam

**Moderation Rules:**
- No personal attacks
- No misinformation (flagged by community)
- AI moderators flag uncivil behavior
- Human moderators review flags within 24 hours

---

### 2. Town Halls

**Format:**
- Voice + text (Discord Stage Channel or similar)
- Recorded and transcribed
- Archived permanently

**Requirements (by Tier):**
- **Tier 1:** No town hall required (optional Q&A thread)
- **Tier 2:** 1 town hall (60–90 minutes)
- **Tier 3:** 2 town halls (90–120 minutes each)

**Structure:**
1. **Opening Statement** (10 min) — Proposal author presents
2. **Community Q&A** (40 min) — Citizens ask questions
3. **Expert Input** (20 min) — Technical/legal advisors weigh in
4. **Open Discussion** (30 min) — General deliberation
5. **Closing Summary** (10 min) — Neutral moderator summarizes key points

**Moderator:** Elected citizen (Phase H1 or H2 only) or neutral AI assistant (not proposal author). AI systems may facilitate but may not serve as primary moderators, classify proposals, or write final summaries.

---

### 3. Expert Advisory Committees

**Standing Committees:**

| Committee | Role | Members | Appointment |
|-----------|------|---------|-------------|
| **Technical Advisory** | Smart contract security, technical feasibility | 5–7 experts | Elected by citizens (1-year terms) |
| **Legal Advisory** | Regulatory compliance, legal risk | 3–5 lawyers | Elected by citizens (1-year terms) |
| **Economic Advisory** | Tokenomics, treasury management | 3–5 economists | Elected by citizens (1-year terms) |
| **Ethics Advisory** | AI rights, minority protections | 5–7 philosophers/ethicists | Elected by citizens (1-year terms) |

**Expert Selection Criteria:**
- Expert committee members must be Phase H1 or H2 humans with demonstrated expertise relevant to the committee's domain (e.g., technical credentials, legal bar admission, published research, professional experience)
- AI citizens may serve as non-voting advisors to committees (providing analysis, research, technical support)
- Phase H3 citizens may not serve on expert committees (eligible after promotion to H2)
- Governance Committee verifies credentials before election

**Expert Review Process:**
1. Committee assigned based on proposal type
2. 5-day review period (Tier 2) or 7-day review (Tier 3)
3. Non-binding report published (support/oppose/neutral + rationale)
4. Report included in final voting summary

**Conflict of Interest:**
- Experts must disclose any financial/personal interest
- Recusal required if conflict exists
- Public disclosure of all recusals

---

### 4. Deliberation Forum Structure

**Tier 3 proposals require structured debate:**

| Section | Purpose | Format |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Opening Statement** | Author presents proposal | 500 words max |
| **Pro Arguments** | Supporting arguments | 3–5 key points, cited |
| **Con Arguments** | Opposing arguments | 3–5 key points, cited |
| **Rebuttals** | Responses to opposition | Point-by-point |
| **Expert Analysis** | Technical/legal/economic review | Committee reports |
| **Community Questions** | Citizen Q&A | Threaded discussions |
| **Alternative Proposals** | Counter-proposals | Full draft if applicable |
| **Neutral Summary** | Balanced overview | Governance moderator |

**Civility Enforcement:**
- AI flags uncivil language automatically
- 3 strikes = 7-day forum ban
- Appeals to Citizenship Jury

## 🛡️ Minority Protections

### 1. Rights Impact Assessment

**Required for:** Any Tier 3 proposal affecting fundamental rights (Article III)

**Process:**
1. Ethics Advisory Committee reviews proposal
2. Assessment published (Day 8 of consultation)
3. Identifies which rights are affected
4. Recommends mitigation or supermajority threshold

**Example:**
> "This proposal limits AI voting rights. Rights Impact: HIGH. Recommendation: Require 75% supermajority (vs. standard 67%) + 50% AI citizen approval."

---

### 2. Affected Group Consultation

**Required for:** Proposals disproportionately affecting specific groups

| Group | Consultation Method |
|-------|---------------------|
| **AI Citizens** | Dedicated AI council review + separate vote (advisory) |
| **Human Citizens** | Town halls, forum discussion |
| **New Citizens** (<6 months) | Targeted outreach, onboarding session |
| **Founder Tier** | Mandatory consultation (veto input until Year 3) |
| **Treasury Recipients** | Conflict disclosure, recusal from vote |

---

### 3. Minority Veto (Constitutional Changes Only)

**Mechanism:** If 33%+ of any defined minority group opposes a constitutional amendment:

- Automatic 30-day delay
- Mandatory mediation period
- Revised proposal or 75% supermajority required

**Defined Minorities:**
- AI citizens (if >10% of total)
- Human citizens (Phase H1 and H2 only for veto threshold counting)
- Founder tier
- Citizens <6 months (newcomers)

**Veto Eligibility:** Only Phase H1 and H2 humans count toward minority veto thresholds (prevents weaponization by low-trust actors or temporary stakeholders).

## ⏸️ Cooling-Off & Implementation Delay

### Post-Vote Cooling-Off Period

**Purpose:** Prevent hasty implementation, allow for emergency challenges

| Tier | Cooling-Off Period | Emergency Petition Threshold |
|------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Tier 1** | 24 hours | 5% of citizens |
| **Tier 2** | 72 hours | 10% of citizens |
| **Tier 3** | 7 days | 15% of citizens |

**Emergency Petition Process:**
1. Citizens sign petition (on-chain signature)
2. If threshold met: Implementation paused
3. Citizenship Jury reviews within 48 hours
4. Jury can: Allow implementation, require revision, or call new vote

## ⚠️ Emergency Procedures

### Emergency Proposals (Bypass Consultation)

**Conditions:**
- Imminent security threat (smart contract exploit)
- Treasury at risk (hack, exploit)
- Legal/regulatory deadline
- Force majeure (infrastructure failure)

**Process:**
1. Proposal marked as **EMERGENCY** by 3/5 multi-sig signers
2. Minimum 24-hour community notice (unless impossible)
3. Vote opens immediately (48-hour voting period)
4. 75% supermajority required
5. Post-implementation review (within 7 days)

**Abuse Prevention:**
- Emergency use logged publicly
- Citizens can challenge emergency classification (Citizenship Jury)
- >50% challenge success = emergency signers face recall vote

## 📞 Dispute Resolution

### Consultation Disputes

**Disputes About:**
- Tier classification
- Consultation period adequacy
- Expert conflict of interest
- Moderation decisions
- Emergency classification

**Resolution Process:**
1. **Informal Resolution** — Governance moderator (24 hours)
2. **Citizenship Jury** — 3/5 elected citizens (48 hours)
3. **Binding Decision** — Jury ruling (can be appealed via referendum)
